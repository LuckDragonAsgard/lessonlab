③ELEMENT 3 — EXPLICIT TEACHING

VTLM 2.0
②
EXPLICIT TEACHING — I DO 8 minutes
Focus: [Key concept / skill being explicitly taught — I Do approach with think-aloud]
1
Activate prior knowledge: "Has anyone done a chest pass before? What do you think we need to do?" — gather 2–3 ideas. Validate and redirect to cue cards on the board.
2
Demonstrate with think-aloud: Hold ball at chest, elbows out (READY). Step toward target (STEP). Extend both arms and push (PUSH). Fingers point toward target on release (FOLLOW). Repeat slowly ×2.
3
Whole class mirror: Students stand, mirror all 4 cues in slow-motion without a ball ×2. Teacher narrates: READY → STEP → PUSH → FOLLOW. Students call out each cue as they mirror.
4
Partner slow-motion: Each pair gets 1 ball. One student does a slow-motion chest pass; partner gives thumbs up when they see all 3 cues. Swap roles once each.
📌 Teaching Notes
Common errors to address: (a) throwing overhead — cue: READY (ball at shirt buttons) — cue "ball starts at your shirt buttons," (b) not extending arms — cue "reach and release," (c) stepping sideways — cue "step toward your target."
Use visual cue card on whiteboard throughout lesson.
❓ Check for Understanding
Thumbs signal: "Show me your READY position." Scan the group — look for elbows at shoulder height, ball at chest. Address any misconceptions before moving to guided practice.
→ Transition: "One ball between two. Find a partner and stand on opposite blue lines — 3 metres apart."
