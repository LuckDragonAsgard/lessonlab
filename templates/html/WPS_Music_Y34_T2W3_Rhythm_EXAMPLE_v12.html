Westbourne Park School  |  Music

Year 3/4
Term 2 Week 3 — 2026

VC2MUSC2301 – Elements of Music Year 3/4 45 min Music Room 26 students
📋 CRT Instructions — Lesson Overview
Focus / Topic
Steady Beat & Rhythm Patterns
Resources
Rhythm cards, untuned percussion, whiteboards
Room / Setup
Circle on mat → instrument stations
Learning Intention
Keep a steady beat; read & perform rhythm patterns
Learning Intention & Success Criteria
We are learning to identify and perform steady beat and rhythm patterns using body percussion and untuned instruments.  I can: (1) keep a steady beat while performing, (2) identify quarter notes (ta) and eighth notes (ti-ti) in 4-beat patterns, (3) read and clap a rhythm pattern from a card.
Opening
① OPENING / ACTIVATION
5 min
Body percussion warm-up — activate students' sense of steady beat and energise the group
1
Begin a 4-beat steady clap. Invite students to join in. Vary dynamics (loud/soft) and tempo (faster/slower) responding to student energy.
2
Layer in stomp on beat 1 and clap on beats 2–4. Call-and-response: teacher claps a short rhythm, students echo.
3
Ask: "What stayed the same?" (the beat). "What changed?" (the rhythm on top). 2–3 hands-up responses.
📌 NOTE  📌 Watch for students who clap the rhythm instead of the beat — these are your focus students. Seat them near you during teaching.
→ "Let's look at how musicians write down rhythm. Sit on the mat facing the board."
Explicit Teaching
② EXPLICIT TEACHING
8 min
Explicit notation — reading and performing quarter notes (ta) and eighth notes (ti-ti) using rhythm cards
1
Show large rhythm card: 4 quarter notes. "One note = one beat — we say ta." Clap together: ta-ta-ta-ta. Repeat ×2.
2
Show two eighth notes beamed. "Two eighth notes fill one beat — we say ti-ti." Clap: ti-ti-ti-ti (fast). Then: ta ti-ti ta ta. Repeat ×3.
3
Clap a 4-beat mixed pattern. Students mirror. Then point to rhythm card showing the same pattern. "Can you read this before I clap it?"
❓ CFU  ❓ Hold up a new rhythm card. Students silently clap on their lap, then clap aloud together. Scan — who hesitates on ti-ti?
→ "Pick up the card in front of you. Find a partner near you."
We Do
③ WE DO (GUIDED)
10 min
Partner rhythm reading — students practise reading cards and echoing each other, teacher circulates with feedback
1
Partner A claps their rhythm card (4 beats), Partner B echoes. Swap cards and roles.
2
Move to a new partner. Clap your pattern; ask partner to write ta/ti-ti notation on their mini whiteboard.
3
Group of 4: each claps a different card simultaneously. Listen — does it work together? Teacher nominates one group to share.
📌 NOTE  📌 Students rush eighth notes — cue: "ti-ti fits inside the SAME space as ta." Use metronome app on tablet if needed.
→ "Return cards to the pile. Move to your instrument station — look for your name card."
You Do
④ YOU DO (INDEPENDENT)
15 min
Instrument stations — independent application of rhythm reading on untuned percussion
1
Station A — Clap & Write: read 3 rhythm cards, write each pattern using ta/ti-ti on the recording sheet.
2
Station B — Play the Pattern: choose a card, perform it on drum, woodblock, or claves. Record which patterns you played.
3
Station C — Create: compose your own 4-beat rhythm using ta and ti-ti. Write it on a blank card and practise performing it.
❓ CFU  ❓ Tap 3–4 students during stations: "Clap your pattern for me." Look for steady beat and accurate ti-ti.
→ "Return instruments to shelf quietly. Back to the mat."
Closure
⑤ CLOSURE & REFLECTION
7 min
Exit reflection — demonstrate understanding of beat vs rhythm; self-assess against success criteria
1
Students return cards and recording sheets. Instruments to shelf.
2
Exit ticket: teacher claps two patterns — one is the steady beat, one is a rhythm. "Which is which?" Thumbs up = first, thumbs down = second.
3
Preview: "Next lesson we'll use pitched instruments to turn your rhythm pattern into a 2-part ostinato."
📦 Resources & Materials
  • Rhythm cards — sets of 10 (quarter/eighth combinations)
  • Untuned percussion: drums, claves, woodblocks, tambourines
  • Mini whiteboards and dry-erase markers
  • Station recording sheets (printed)
  • Metronome app (tablet)
⚠️ Safety & Wellbeing Notes
  • Instruments handled carefully — no hitting other students with mallets
  • Agree on a stop/freeze signal before stations begin (raised hand + "3-2-1")
  • Check for hearing sensitivities — seat affected students away from drums
  • Students with fine motor difficulties: use a larger drum rather than claves
  • Volume management — signal if room noise exceeds comfortable level

Learning Intention
We are learning to identify and perform steady beat and rhythm patterns using body percussion and untuned percussion instruments.
Success Criteria
I can: (1) keep a steady beat independently while performing, (2) identify and name quarter notes (ta) and eighth notes (ti-ti) in a 4-beat pattern, (3) read a rhythm pattern from a card and perform it accurately.
Victorian Curriculum Links
VC2MUSC2301 — Explore the use of rhythm in music they listen to and make, including rhythm patterns. VC2MUSC2302 — Practise and rehearse music they create and perform.
Class Profile / Context
26 students (Y3/Y4 split). 3 EAL/D students (Vietnamese, Mandarin). 2 students with IEPs — fine motor focus. Most students have experienced beat activities in Yr 1/2.
Evidence from Last Lesson
Last lesson: students could maintain a steady beat with clapping but struggled to layer rhythm on top. 6 students still confusing beat and rhythm — targeted today.
Victorian Curriculum 2.0 Achievement Standard — Year 3/4 Music
By the end of Year 4, students perform music using rhythm, pitch and dynamics. They identify and describe elements of music including rhythm, pitch, dynamics and form. Students use music concepts to compose simple pieces and explain how composers use the elements of music.

①ELEMENT 1 — PLANNING FOR LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
Prior Knowledge / Pre-Assessment
Students have maintained a steady beat in Years F–2. Most can echo clap simple 2-beat patterns. Limited exposure to formal notation (ta/ti-ti).
Differentiation Planning
Tier 1 (Supported): Use 2-beat patterns on rhythm cards; partner with strong beat-keeper; trace notation on card. Core: Standard 4-beat patterns as planned. Tier 3 (Extension): Create 8-beat patterns; compose a 4-bar piece using ta/ti-ti; notate in rhythm grid.
Links to Sequence / Unit Overview
Lesson 3 of 8 in "Beat, Rhythm and Notation" unit. Builds toward creating and performing a 4-bar ostinato in Lesson 6. Links to VC2MUSC2301.
Resources & Space Required
Music room set up with instrument stations (3 × stations around the room). Whiteboard with rhythm cards attached using magnets. Tablets at Station B.

②ELEMENT 2 — ENABLING LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
①
OPENING / ACTIVATION 5 minutes
Focus: Body percussion call-and-response to activate students' sense of steady beat and distinguish beat from rhythm before notation is introduced.
1
[Opening step 1] Begin a 4-beat steady clap at a moderate tempo (~70bpm). Count students in: "Ready — 1 2 3 4." Students join in.
2
[Opening step 2] Layer in stomp on beat 1 while clapping beats 2–4. Call-and-response echo clapping: teacher claps a 4-beat rhythm, students echo.
3
[Opening step 3] Bring class together: "What stayed the same? What changed?" Draw out vocabulary — beat (steady pulse) vs rhythm (pattern on top).
📌 Teacher Note
Differentiation: Students who cannot yet distinguish beat from rhythm are seated closest to you. Use physical cue — tap shoulder on beat 1 to anchor their sense of pulse.
→ Transition: "Sit on the mat facing the board — I want you to see the rhythm cards clearly."

③ELEMENT 3 — EXPLICIT TEACHING

VTLM 2.0
②
EXPLICIT TEACHING — I DO 8 minutes
Focus: Explicit notation — introducing quarter notes (ta) and eighth notes (ti-ti) through visual cards, teacher modelling, and mirroring.
1
Activate prior knowledge: "Has anyone seen music written down? What does it look like?" 2–3 responses. Show a simple 4-beat rhythm card. "Today we learn to READ rhythm."
2
Quarter notes (ta): Point to card showing ♩♩♩♩. "Each of these gets one beat — we say ta." Model: tap chest once per note. "Join me: ta-ta-ta-ta." Repeat ×3, varying tempo.
3
Eighth notes (ti-ti): Show ♪♪ beamed together. "Two eighth notes share one beat — we say ti-ti." Model ×2. Then mix: "ta ti-ti ta ta." Students mirror. Compare the feel of rushing vs accurate ti-ti.
4
Read before you clap: Show a new 4-beat card. "Look first — read silently in your head." 5 seconds. "Now clap with me." Students read, then perform.
📌 Teaching Notes
Key misconceptions: (a) rushing ti-ti — cue "ti-ti fits INSIDE ta's space," (b) clapping ta as two claps — cue "one note, one clap." Keep visual cards visible throughout lesson. Tap metronome for students who rush.
❓ Check for Understanding
Show a new card — students silently clap on their lap (no sound). Scan for accuracy. Then "1-2-3 — clap!" together. If more than 6 students make errors, reteach the problem notation before proceeding.
→ Transition: "Excellent. Pick up the card in front of you — yours has your name on the back. Find a partner."

④ELEMENT 4 — SUPPORTED APPLICATION

VTLM 2.0
③
WE DO — GUIDED PRACTICE 10 minutes
Focus: Guided partner practice — students read rhythm cards and echo each other with teacher circulating and providing targeted feedback.
1
Partner A reads and claps their rhythm card (4 beats). Partner B echoes exactly. Check with each other. Swap cards and roles.
2
New partner. Clap your card. Partner writes what they hear on their mini whiteboard (ta or ti-ti for each beat). Compare — do they match the card?
3
Group of 4: each claps a different card at the same time. Listen for how the parts combine. Teacher stops group mid-way: "Did everyone stay together?"
❓ Checking for Understanding
Stop activity: "Show me READY position for ta." Students hold one finger up. "Show me ti-ti." Two fingers. Quick visual scan for understanding before stations.
→ Transition: "Return cards to the centre pile. Look for your station sign — Station A, B, or C — and move there now."
📊 Differentiated Station Tasks — Element 4 Application
🔴 Tier 1 — Supported 🔵 Core Task 🟢 Tier 3 — Extension
Reduce pattern length to 2 beats. Partner work only (no solo). Provide visual anchor card showing ta = 1 clap, ti-ti = 2 fast claps. 4-beat patterns using ta and ti-ti combinations as designed. Read from card and perform independently. 8-beat patterns. Create their own pattern. Begin composing a 4-bar piece. Write patterns using rhythm grid (instead of ta/ti-ti labels).
Station A (Supported): Clap 2-beat patterns with teacher aide. Use large print cards. Trace notation with finger as you clap. Stations A–C (Core): Rotate through all 3 stations as designed. Complete recording sheet with at least 2 patterns per station. Station C (Extension): Compose an 8-beat rhythm. Write it in a rhythm grid. Teach it to a Year 3 student nearby. Create a second contrasting pattern.
④
YOU DO — INDEPENDENT APPLICATION 15 minutes
Focus: Independent application — students work at instrument stations without direct guidance while teacher assesses rhythm reading accuracy.
1
Station A — Clap & Write: Read 3 rhythm cards. Write each pattern using ta/ti-ti notation on recording sheet. Self-check: clap your written version back against the card.
2
Station B — Play the Pattern: Choose a rhythm card. Perform it on drum, woodblock, or claves. Try 3 different cards. Circle your favourite on recording sheet.
3
Station C — Create a Pattern: Compose your own 4-beat rhythm using ta and ti-ti. Write it on a blank card. Practise until you can clap it steadily 3 times in a row.
📌 Assessment Observation Window
Assessing VC2MUSC2301 — rhythm. Target 6–8 students. Look for: accurate ti-ti timing, steady tempo, correct number of beats. Record on class checklist. Use anecdotal notes for standout observations.
→ Transition: "Timer going off in 10 seconds — instruments to shelf, return to mat. Bring your recording sheet."

⑤ELEMENT 5 — REFLECTION ON LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
⑤
PACK UP & STUDENT REFLECTION 7 minutes
1
Students return instruments to shelf and recording sheets to teacher tray. 30-second tidy-up.
2
Exit ticket: teacher claps — "Is this a steady beat or a rhythm?" Two options, students vote. Then: "Clap a 4-beat pattern that has at least one ti-ti."
3
Preview: "Next lesson we add pitched glockenspiels and turn our rhythm into a 2-part ostinato — very cool." Dismiss by table group.
❓ Exit Reflection
Did students demonstrate beat vs rhythm distinction? How many correctly identified ti-ti on the exit clap? Were there any students who need a 1:1 reteach before next lesson?
💚 Wellbeing & Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)
Wellbeing Focus
Growth mindset in music — normalise the difficulty of learning new notation. "Mistakes are how our brain grows." Celebrate risk-taking when students share their created patterns.
Social Skill Target
Partner and group work requires respectful listening — model active listening body language. Echo activities build turn-taking. Discuss: what does it feel like to have your partner listen carefully?
Emotional Regulation
Music volume can escalate quickly at stations. Use the visual stop signal (raised hand) before it becomes dysregulating. Provide a quiet corner with headphones for any student who needs low-stimulus time.
♿ Inclusive Practice & Adjustments
EAL/D Students
EAL/D students (3): visual rhythm cards with ta/ti-ti labels reduce language load. Partner with patient English-proficient peer. Gesture and model rather than verbal explanation where possible.
Learning / Physical Support
2 students with IEPs (fine motor): offer larger drum head instead of claves. Tapping with flat hand is acceptable alternative to clapping. Reduce card set to 5 cards max.
Extension / Gifted
Extension: 8-beat composition task at Station C. Encourage these students to teach their pattern to others — a strong peer-teaching opportunity within the music context.
Medical / Withdrawal Notes
Check register: no known hearing impairments this class. Any student returning from illness — reduce percussion volume exposure. One student wears hearing aids — seat away from drum station.
📊 Assessment & Evidence
Assessment Strategy
Observation during stations — clipboard checklist with class list. Look for: steady beat maintained while reading, accurate ti-ti timing, independently reads a new card. Exit clap for whole-class snapshot.
