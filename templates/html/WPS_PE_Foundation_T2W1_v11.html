Westbourne Park School  |  Physical Education

Term 2, Week 1 — 2025
Mr. P. Gallivan
VC2HPFM01 – Movement & Physical Activity Foundation 45 min Gym / Oval 30 students
📋 CRT Instructions — Lesson Overview
Focus Skill
Object Control – Chest Pass
Equipment
Cones, bibs, balls ×15
Space
Full oval / gym floor
Learning Intention
Move safely & follow rules
Learning Intention & Success Criteria
We are learning to travel safely in shared spaces and follow game rules.  I can: (1) move safely & follow signals, (2) name the 4 cues (READY–STEP–PUSH–FOLLOW), (3) coach a partner using at least 1 cue.
Warm Up
① WARM UP
5 min
Get hearts pumping — Space Invaders tag game. Students move freely; tagged players do 5 star jumps.
1
Set boundary with 4 cones. Choose 2 taggers, give them a bib.
2
Play Space Invaders: taggers freeze runners with a gentle shoulder touch.
3
Rotate taggers every 60 sec. Swap on teacher signal (clap ×2).
📌 NOTE  If gym — reduce boundary to half-court. Watch for students running into walls.
→ "Freeze! Hands on head. Walk quietly to the middle circle and sit down facing me."
Explicit Teaching
② EXPLICIT TEACHING
8 min
Demonstrate key movement skill — chest pass technique (ready position, step & push, follow through).
1
Ask: "What does a good pass look like?" — gather ideas.
2
Model: READY (ball at chest, elbows out) → STEP (forward foot) → PUSH (extend both arms) → FOLLOW (fingers point to target).
3
Class mirrors you 3×. Pair students to practise slow-motion once each.
❓ CFU  "Show me your READY position." Scan — look for elbows up and ball at chest height.
→ "Take one ball between two and find a space on the blue line."
We Do
③ WE DO (GUIDED)
10 min
Partner passing practice with teacher feedback — stationary, then step forward on catch.
1
Pairs stand 3 m apart and pass back and forth ×5. CRT circulates — cue "PUSH and FOLLOW."
2
Increase distance to 5 m. Students self-assess: thumbs up if pass reached partner at chest height.
3
3-person triangle passes — one rotation, then swap roles.
📌 NOTE  Common error: students throwing overhead. Remind: "Keep it low, push from chest."
→ "Balls in the bucket. Rotate to your station colour — check the board for which station is yours."
You Do
④ YOU DO (INDEPENDENT)
15 min
Station rotations — students apply skills independently while CRT monitors all stations.
1
Station A (Red): Target passing — hit the hoop on the wall ×3 then swap.
2
Station B (Blue): Keep-away — 4v1, team keeps ball from defender for 30 sec.
3
Station C (Green): Relay passing — line of 4, chest pass down and back.
❓ CFU  Visit each station. Ask: "What's one thing you're focusing on?" — listen for "push" or "follow through."
→ "Equipment down. Walk to the finish circle and sit quietly."
Pack Up
⑤ PACK UP & REFLECTION
7 min
Return equipment, cool down, student self-reflection against success criteria.
1
Group returns equipment to storage. Assign 2 students as "equipment managers."
2
Seated circle — thumbs up/down: "Did you use a chest pass today?"
3
Students share: "Next time I will try to…" Line up quietly for class return.
📦 Equipment List
  • 15× foam balls
  • 20× marker cones
  • 4× bibs / vests
  • 2× target hoops (wall mount)
  • 3× ball buckets
⚠️ Safety & Inclusion
  • Foam balls only — no hard balls
  • Clear all trip hazards before start
  • Check medical register (office copy)
  • Adjust station distances for needs
  • Signal: clap ×2 = FREEZE immediately

Foundation
Term 2, Week 1 — Chest Pass
Mr. P. Gallivan
Learning Intention
We are learning to perform a chest pass using correct technique: READY, STEP, PUSH, FOLLOW, and to apply this in game situations with peers.
Success Criteria
I can: (1) demonstrate all 4 cues (READY, STEP, PUSH, FOLLOW) in sequence, (2) chest pass accurately to a partner at 3–5m, (3) identify a cue to improve when watching a peer.
Victorian Curriculum Links
VC2HPFM01 – Fundamental movement skills. VC2HPFP01 – Personal & social skills through movement.
Class Profile / Context
30 students. 3 EAL/D. 2 students with fine motor support plans. Mixed prior experience with ball sports. Enthusiastic class with high engagement in games.
Evidence from Last Lesson
Students showed strong spatial awareness in tag games. Passing technique was inconsistent — most students pushed from shoulder rather than chest. Targeting this in today's explicit teaching.
Victorian Curriculum 2.0 Achievement Standard — Foundation
By end of Foundation, students perform fundamental movement skills in a variety of movement sequences and situations. They incorporate the elements of movement when composing and performing movement sequences. They use personal and social skills to cooperate with others and to include others in activities.

①ELEMENT 1 — PLANNING FOR LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
Prior Knowledge / Pre-Assessment
Observed in Week 10 last term: majority of students have experienced ball passing in play. Several students could not catch at chest height reliably. Class enjoys competitive game formats.
Differentiation Planning
Tier 1: Use larger/lighter ball, reduce distance to 2m, peer buddy support.
Core: Standard task as written.
Tier 3: Increase distance, add a defender, or use non-dominant hand challenge.
Links to Sequence / Unit Overview
Lesson 1 of 4 in "Passing & Ball Control" unit. Builds toward lead-up netball game in Week 4. Foundation for VC2HPFM01 assessment task.
Resources & Space Required
15× foam balls, 20× cones, 4× bibs, 2× target hoops, full oval or gym half-court. Ball pump available in PE storeroom.

②ELEMENT 2 — ENABLING LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
①
WARM UP & ACTIVATION 5 minutes
Focus: Raise heart rate, activate spatial awareness and listening — Space Invaders tag game in boundary area.
1
Set boundary with 4 cones (approx 20m × 20m for oval; half-court for gym). Choose 2 taggers and give them a bib. Explain: taggers gently touch shoulder to freeze a runner.
2
Play Space Invaders: frozen students stand still until a free runner does 5 star jumps next to them to "unfreeze." Rotate taggers every 60 seconds on signal.
3
Cool transition: Call FREEZE (clap ×2). Students walk 2 laps of boundary, shaking out arms. Dynamic stretches: arm circles, wrist rolls, side twists ×5 each.
📌 Teacher Note
Check for students who need modified movement — allow walking or reduced boundary for those with physical needs. Watch for wall proximity in gym setting.
→ Transition: "Freeze! Hands on head. Walk quietly to the middle circle and sit down facing me."

③ELEMENT 3 — EXPLICIT TEACHING

VTLM 2.0
②
EXPLICIT TEACHING — I DO 8 minutes
Focus: Teach the 3-cue chest pass model (READY → PUSH → FOLLOW THROUGH) using demonstration, think-aloud, and guided practice.
1
Activate prior knowledge: "Has anyone done a chest pass before? What do you think we need to do?" — gather 2–3 ideas. Validate and redirect to cue cards on the board.
2
Demonstrate with think-aloud: Hold ball at chest, elbows out (READY). Step toward target (STEP). Extend both arms and push (PUSH). Fingers point toward target on release (FOLLOW). Repeat slowly ×2.
3
Whole class mirror: Students stand, mirror all 4 cues in slow-motion without a ball ×2. Teacher narrates: READY → STEP → PUSH → FOLLOW. Students call out each cue as they mirror.
4
Partner slow-motion: Each pair gets 1 ball. One student does a slow-motion chest pass; partner gives thumbs up when they see all 3 cues. Swap roles once each.
📌 Teaching Notes
Common errors to address: (a) throwing overhead — cue: READY (ball at shirt buttons) — cue "ball starts at your shirt buttons," (b) not extending arms — cue "reach and release," (c) stepping sideways — cue "step toward your target."
Use visual cue card on whiteboard throughout lesson.
❓ Check for Understanding
Thumbs signal: "Show me your READY position." Scan the group — look for elbows at shoulder height, ball at chest. Address any misconceptions before moving to guided practice.
→ Transition: "One ball between two. Find a partner and stand on opposite blue lines — 3 metres apart."

④ELEMENT 4 — SUPPORTED APPLICATION

VTLM 2.0
③
WE DO — GUIDED PRACTICE 10 minutes
Focus: Guided partner passing — teacher provides corrective feedback, students self-assess using cue card.
1
Pairs at 3m: 5 passes each, partner gives feedback using the 3 cues ("READY ✓, PUSH ✓, FOLLOW ✗"). Teacher circulates with targeted feedback — prioritise students flagged in pre-assessment.
2
Increase distance to 5m. Students self-assess: was the pass at chest height? Thumbs up/middle/down. Switch partners after 3 passes for broader peer interaction.
3
Triangle passes (groups of 3): one rotation of 6 passes total. Challenge: try to complete triangle without the ball bouncing. Teacher observes and names good technique publicly.
❓ Checking for Understanding
Stop class at halfway. "Show me — which cue is hardest for you?" Quick scan and class discussion. Reteach FOLLOW THROUGH if needed using one student as model.
→ Transition: "Balls in the bucket. Rotate to your station colour — check the board for which station is yours."
📊 Differentiated Station Tasks — Element 4 Application
🔴 Tier 1 — Supported 🔵 Core Task 🟢 Tier 3 — Extension
Use larger foam ball (size 5→7). Reduce distance to 2m. Peer buddy provides verbal cue before each pass. May perform stationary slow-motion pass without stepping. Standard task: 5 passes at 3m, increase to 5m. Triangle passing. Self-assess with cue card. Focus on all 3 technical cues with consistency. Pass to moving partner (catcher jogs sideways). Use non-dominant hand for 2 passes. Bonus: call "go" before each pass so receiver must react and reposition.
Station A (modified): Target hoop at 1.5m, may bounce ball once. Support from aide or buddy. Station B: 4v1 keep-away, standard chest pass only. Rotate defender every 30 sec. Station C (extension): Relay with bounce pass alternate — chest, bounce, chest, bounce sequence.
④
YOU DO — INDEPENDENT APPLICATION 15 minutes
Focus: 3-station rotation — students apply technique independently while teacher observes for assessment evidence.
1
Station A – Target Passing (Red cones): Pass at wall-mounted hoop from 3m. Score 1 point per clean pass through. Record personal score. Rotate after 5 attempts each.
2
Station B – Keep Away (Blue cones): 4v1. Team must use chest pass only — no bouncing or overhead. Defender tries to intercept within 30 sec. Rotate defender role.
3
Station C – Relay (Green cones): Line of 4 students. Chest pass down the line and back. Team counts total successful passes in 60 sec. Challenge own score each rotation.
📌 Assessment Observation Window
Use this block to gather anecdotal evidence for VC2HPFM01. Target 6–8 students per lesson for observation notes. Record on clipboard: student name + cue observed (R/P/F). Note: who demonstrates consistently, who needs reteaching.
→ Transition: "Equipment down where it is. Walk to the finish circle and sit cross-legged. No talking until everyone is seated."

⑤ELEMENT 5 — REFLECTION ON LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
⑤
PACK UP & STUDENT REFLECTION 7 minutes
1
Equipment managers (2 students) collect balls and cones while class completes cool-down stretches (arms across chest, wrist flexor stretch, slow neck rolls ×5).
2
Seated circle reflection — "Traffic light": Green = I nailed my chest pass today / Amber = I'm getting there / Red = I need more practice. Students hold up a finger (1/2/3).
3
Pair share: "Tell your partner one thing you will focus on next lesson." 2 volunteers share with class. Teacher closes: "Next lesson we'll add movement — catching from a bounce pass."
❓ Exit Reflection
Students give thumbs up/down: "Did we achieve our learning intention today?" Teacher notes patterns for planning next lesson.
💚 Wellbeing & Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)
Wellbeing Focus
Positive self-talk during skill learning. Acknowledge "not yet" mindset — mistakes are part of learning. Celebrate effort, not just outcome.
Social Skill Target
Respectful feedback to partners — "I noticed your arms…" framing. Encourage and include — no negative comments about peers' attempts.
Emotional Regulation
Students who struggle with frustration: offer a "reset moment" — step to blue cone, do 5 deep breaths, rejoin. Normalise this as a strategy, not a punishment.
♿ Inclusive Practice & Adjustments
EAL/D Students (3)
Use visual cue cards with diagrams rather than text. Pair with supportive English-speaking buddy. Demonstrate each station personally before group starts.
Fine Motor Support (2 students)
Larger ball (size 7 foam). Reduced grip — may hold ball from underneath rather than sides. Check IEP for specific strategies. Aide support during We Do block.
Extension / Gifted
Assign as peer coach role at Station C. Challenge: pass with non-dominant hand, add a jump before release, time the relay for personal best record.
Medical / Withdrawal Notes
Check class medical register (office). Students with asthma: ensure puffer accessible. Students withdrawn from vigorous activity may still participate in station observation and peer coaching roles.
📊 Assessment & Evidence
Assessment Strategy
Anecdotal observation during You Do block (targeted 6–8 students). Clipboard checklist: READY ✓/✗, PUSH ✓/✗, FOLLOW ✓/✗. Traffic light self-assessment at end of lesson for whole class data. Photos of students at Station A with consent.
