📋 Visual Art Lesson Plan — CRT Quick Reference

Westbourne Park School  |  Visual Art

Year 5/6
Term 2 Week 2 — 2026
P. Gallivan
VC2VSAR2501 – Visual Arts Year 5/6 50 min Art Room 24 students
📋 CRT Instructions — Lesson Overview
Focus / Topic
Observational Drawing — Contour Line & Tone
Resources
Drawing pencils (HB, 2B, 4B), natural objects, cartridge paper
Room / Setup
Art tables with individual still-life object at each seat
Learning Intention
Draw what you see; use line quality and tone to show form
Learning Intention & Success Criteria
We are learning to observe carefully and use contour line and tonal shading to represent a natural object realistically.  I can: (1) draw slow, confident contour lines that follow the edges and surface of my object, (2) apply 3 values of tone (light, mid, dark) using graduated pencil pressure, (3) use the technique of "draw what you see, not what you think you know."
Opening
① OPENING / ACTIVATION
5 min
Look & Think & Wonder — activate careful observation and curiosity about the object before drawing begins
1
Distribute one natural object per student (shell, leaf, seed pod, stone). "Don't draw yet. Just look. Turn it in your hands for 60 seconds."
2
Think-Pair-Share: "Describe your object to your partner without naming it." Focus on shape, texture, edges, light and shadow.
3
Display 2–3 examples of botanical illustration (projected). "What do you notice about how this artist drew these? What makes it look real?"
📌 NOTE  📌 Resist students rushing to draw. The quality of observation determines the quality of the drawing. Make this 8 minutes feel worthwhile.
→ "Now we're going to learn the technique artists use to draw from observation. Keep your object in front of you."
Explicit Teaching
② EXPLICIT TEACHING
8 min
Explicit demonstration — contour line drawing (drawing the edges and surface structure without lifting the pencil)
1
Hold a shell at the front. "I'm going to draw the outline — but I'm going to move my pencil at the SAME SPEED that my eye moves along the edge." Demonstrate slow contour line on large paper.
2
Think aloud: "I'm not looking at my paper much — I'm looking at my object. My hand is following what my eye sees." Demonstrate again. Students watch.
3
Practice: "Air draw" — students trace the outline of their object in the air with their finger. Then on a scrap piece of paper without looking at the paper.
❓ CFU  ❓ "Hold up your scrap practice. Who surprised themselves? Who wants to try again?" Not a judgement — a diagnostic of confidence.
→ "Take your good cartridge paper. Write your name on the back. Centre your object on the desk in front of you."
We Do
③ WE DO (GUIDED)
10 min
Guided first drawing — students complete a contour outline with teacher circulating and coaching individual technique
1
Students begin contour outline of their object. Teacher circulates: "Move your pencil slowly... keep your eye on the object... slow down at the corners."
2
After 6 minutes: stop the class. "Look at your drawing next to your object. What matches well? What would you change?" 1 minute reflection.
3
Introduce tone: demonstrate HB → 2B → 4B on the side of the paper. "Lighter pencil = lighter tone. More pressure = darker." Students practise a simple tonal scale in the corner of their page.
📌 NOTE  📌 Common errors: drawing the "symbol" for a leaf (pointed oval) rather than the actual leaf shape. Redirect: "Look at THIS edge. What angle does it make HERE?"
→ "You've done the outline — now comes the part that makes it come alive. Keep going on your drawing independently."
You Do
④ YOU DO (INDEPENDENT)
15 min
Independent observational drawing — students complete their drawing with contour and tonal shading, teacher assesses
1
Continue and complete contour line drawing, adding internal structural lines (veins, ridges, surface pattern) observed on the object.
2
Apply tonal shading: identify the lightest area (leave as white/HB), mid-tone areas (2B), darkest shadows (4B). Work from light to dark.
3
Finishing touch: add hatching or cross-hatching in shadow areas if time permits. Sign and date on the front.
❓ CFU  ❓ "Hold up your drawing next to your object. Does the light source match? Where is the darkest shadow?" Quick partner comparison.
→ "Pencils down in 2 minutes. Display your drawing face-up on your desk for gallery walk."
Closure
⑤ CLOSURE & REFLECTION
7 min
Gallery walk and peer reflection — students observe each other's work and give specific, technique-based feedback
1
Gallery walk: students move silently around the room for 3 minutes viewing other drawings. Look for: interesting line quality, effective tone, detail.
2
Leave a sticky note on one drawing (not your own): write ONE specific technique you noticed — e.g. "Great slow contour line along the edge of the spiral."
3
Class discussion: "What did we do differently today compared to drawing from memory?" Connect to LI. Preview: next lesson adds background and composition.
📦 Resources & Materials
  • Drawing pencils — HB, 2B, 4B (one set per student)
  • Natural objects: shells, leaves, seed pods, stones (one per student)
  • A4 cartridge paper (2 sheets per student)
  • Scrap paper for practice
  • Projected botanical illustrations for reference (Google Slides)
⚠️ Safety & Wellbeing Notes
  • Pencils kept on desks — no pointing or flicking
  • Allergy check: handle objects with care if using flowers/seed pods
  • Erasers used gently — no tearing of paper
  • Students with fine motor difficulties: offer larger paper (A3) and softer pencil (4B only)
  • Maintain quiet working atmosphere — respect the concentration of others

24 students (Y5/Y6 split). 2 EAL/D students. 1 student with fine motor IEP. High confidence group — most have done art previously but limited formal technique instruction.

①ELEMENT 1 — PLANNING FOR LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
Prior Knowledge / Pre-Assessment
Students can use pencil for mark-making. Most have not been taught formal contour drawing technique. Basic knowledge of line and shape as art elements.
Differentiation Planning
Tier 1 (Supported): A3 paper, 4B pencil only, simple object (smooth stone). Tracing allowed first time. Core: Standard A4 cartridge, HB/2B/4B, natural object as planned. Tier 3 (Extension): Add cross-hatching; write an artist statement explaining technique choices; attempt a second drawing from a different angle.
Links to Sequence / Unit Overview
Lesson 2 of 6 in "Drawing from Observation" unit. Builds toward a series of 3 related drawings in Lesson 5. Links to VC2VSAR2501.
Resources & Space Required
Art room set with one object per seat before students arrive. Pencil sets distributed on entry. Botanical illustrations loaded in Google Slides. Sticky notes for gallery walk.

②ELEMENT 2 — ENABLING LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
①
OPENING / ACTIVATION 5 minutes
Focus: Slow, mindful observation of natural objects to activate the "seeing" brain before drawing begins — establishing that observation comes before mark-making.
1
Distribute objects as students enter. "Pick it up. Turn it. Look at every part of it for 60 seconds. Don't say anything." Play calm instrumental music.
2
Think-Pair-Share: describe the object to a partner without naming it. Focus vocabulary: edges, curves, texture, shadows, lighter areas.
3
Project botanical illustrations. "What makes this drawing look real? How is it different from a cartoon?" 3–4 responses. Name the techniques: slow line, tonal values.
📌 Teacher Note
The quality of looking directly predicts drawing quality. Resist moving on too quickly. A student who looks well draws well.
→ Transition: "I'm going to show you a technique that artists use. Watch closely — I'm going to draw this shell in front of you."

③ELEMENT 3 — EXPLICIT TEACHING

VTLM 2.0
②
EXPLICIT TEACHING — I DO 8 minutes
Focus: Contour line drawing — moving the pencil at the speed of the eye, following edges rather than creating symbols.
1
Activate prior knowledge: "When you draw a leaf normally, what does it look like?" Students likely draw a teardrop symbol. "That's your IDEA of a leaf — today we draw the ACTUAL leaf in front of us."
2
Demonstrate contour line: Place shell on board tray. "I'm going to trace the edge with my pencil on paper — SLOWLY. My eye moves along the edge, my pencil follows." Demonstrate full contour, mostly looking at the object.
3
Think-aloud tone demo: Show tonal scale on paper (HB, 2B, 4B, heavier pressure). "Artists see 3 things: where the light hits, the middle tones, and the darkest shadows. Your pencil choice creates these."
4
Air drawing practice: "Use your finger to trace the edge of your object in the air — go SLOWLY." Then: "Now do it on scrap paper without looking at your paper." 90 seconds. Celebrate the attempt.
📌 Teaching Notes
Most common errors: (1) drawing too fast — the pencil line looks rushed and wobbly; redirect with "slow down — your eye is ahead of your hand"; (2) symbol drawing — drawing a generic shape; redirect by pointing to a specific edge "draw just THIS part, from here to here."
❓ Check for Understanding
"Hold up your scrap practice. How do you feel about your line?" Thumbs up/middle/down. Address middle/down students: offer to co-draw first few lines with them.
→ Transition: "Take your cartridge paper. Write your name on the back. Centre your object in front of you. Begin."

④ELEMENT 4 — SUPPORTED APPLICATION

VTLM 2.0
③
WE DO — GUIDED PRACTICE 10 minutes
Focus: Teacher-guided contour drawing with check-ins — students draw while teacher coaches individual technique in real time.
1
Students begin contour outline of their object (8 min). Teacher circulates: "Move your eye slowly... where does the line curve?... what angle does it make here?" Kneel to student level.
2
Stop the class (5 min): "Place your drawing next to your object. What do you notice? What does your eye catch that your hand missed?" 1 min reflection, then continue.
3
Introduce tone: class stops again. Demonstrate on a small section: HB (light), 2B (mid), 4B (dark). Students practise tonal scale in corner of their page.
❓ Checking for Understanding
"Point to the lightest area on your object. Point to the darkest shadow. Now draw those two areas first." This gives a structure before independent work.
→ Transition: "You have the outline and you know where your light and dark are. Continue independently — I'm circulating."
📊 Differentiated Station Tasks — Element 4 Application
🔴 Tier 1 — Supported 🔵 Core Task 🟢 Tier 3 — Extension
Simplify object to smooth stone. A3 paper. 4B pencil only. Tracing permitted as first step, then draw from observation on a fresh sheet. A4 cartridge paper. HB, 2B, 4B pencils. Complete contour outline and at least 2 tonal values on the natural object. Complete primary drawing. Add cross-hatching in darkest shadow areas. Write a 3-sentence artist statement explaining technique choices. Attempt a second drawing from a different viewpoint.
Supported: Work with teacher aide on contour of a smooth stone. Trace outlines first, then re-draw from observation. Use only 4B pencil — focus on confidence before accuracy. Core: Complete full contour drawing of natural object with HB/2B/4B tonal shading. Identify 3 tonal zones on their drawing. Extension: Second drawing from alternate angle. Cross-hatching in shadow. 3-sentence artist statement connecting personal choice to technique.
④
YOU DO — INDEPENDENT APPLICATION 15 minutes
Focus: Independent drawing — students complete contour outline and add tonal shading; teacher assesses and takes work samples.
1
Continue contour drawing: Add internal lines — veins, ridges, surface patterns observed on the object. Take your time; a good line takes 30+ seconds per edge.
2
Apply tonal shading: Work from lightest to darkest. Leave the lightest area as HB or white paper. Use 2B for mid-tones, 4B for deepest shadows. Blend gently with finger if desired.
3
Finishing: Step back (hold drawing at arm's length). Does it read as 3D? Add more depth to the shadows if it looks flat. Sign and date on the front bottom-right.
📌 Assessment Observation Window
Assessing VC2VSAR2501 — use of art elements (line, tone). Select 6 drawings for portfolio during gallery walk. Photograph with iPad. Note: quality of line (slow/confident vs rushed/scratchy), tonal range present (yes/no/partial).
→ Transition: "Pencils down in 2 minutes. Place your drawing face-up on your desk — we're going to do a gallery walk."

⑤ELEMENT 5 — REFLECTION ON LEARNING

VTLM 2.0
⑤
PACK UP & STUDENT REFLECTION 7 minutes
1
Pack up pencils into sets. Return objects to the tray at the front. Put name on drawing.
2
Gallery walk (3 min): view each other's work silently. Leave one sticky note on someone else's drawing naming a specific technique you noticed.
3
Class share: "What did slow observation allow you to do that memory drawing couldn't?" Connect to LI. Preview: next lesson adds background and compositional framing.
❓ Exit Reflection
What proportion of students demonstrated slow contour technique (vs symbol drawing)? Who needs technique coaching next lesson? Who is ready for the cross-hatching extension?
💚 Wellbeing & Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)
Wellbeing Focus
Art can trigger vulnerability — some students worry their drawing "isn't good enough." Frame explicitly: "Today we're not making pretty pictures, we're training our eyes to see." Process over product.
Social Skill Target
Gallery walk requires respectful viewing and writing. Model what a specific, kind, technique-focused sticky note looks like vs generic or unkind comments. "I noticed..." sentence starter.
Emotional Regulation
Some students become frustrated when their drawing doesn't match their vision. Validate: "That frustration means you're SEEING accurately — your hands just need more practice. That's exactly why we're here."
♿ Inclusive Practice & Adjustments
EAL/D Students
EAL/D students: visual demonstration removes language barrier for technique. Object-handling and air-drawing are accessible regardless of language. Use gesture and physical modelling.
Learning / Physical Support
Fine motor IEP student: A3 paper, 4B pencil, no expectation for fine detail. Focus assessment on observation quality (can they identify light/dark?) rather than mark precision.
Extension / Gifted
2 students identified as highly capable: cross-hatching extension, second drawing, artist statement. These students mentor peers during gallery walk.
Medical / Withdrawal Notes
Seed pod allergy concern: avoid seed pods for any student with known plant allergies. Shells and stones are safe alternatives.
📊 Assessment & Evidence
Assessment Strategy
Observation during drawing (circulate, note technique quality). Collect drawings as work samples — photograph and return. Sticky notes from gallery walk as peer assessment data.
